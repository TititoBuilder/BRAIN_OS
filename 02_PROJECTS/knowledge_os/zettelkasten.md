# Zettelkasten

## What It Is
Zettelkasten, German for slip-box, is a note-taking method built on small, atomic
notes that link to each other to form a web of knowledge. Instead of long
documents or folders of isolated notes, you write many small notes each capturing
one idea, and connect them, so meaning emerges from the links between ideas as much
as from the ideas themselves.

## How It Works
The method rests on a few principles. Each note holds one idea, atomic, so it can be
linked and reused precisely rather than buried in a long document. Notes are written
in your own words, which forces understanding rather than copying. And notes link to
related notes generously, so following a thread of thought means following links
from note to note. Over time these links accumulate into a graph where related ideas
cluster and unexpected connections surface, the slip-box becomes a thinking partner,
not just storage. New notes attach to existing ones, so knowledge compounds rather
than piling up disconnected.

## Why It Matters
The core insight is that value lives in connections, not in collection. A thousand
isolated notes are a graveyard; a few hundred well-linked atomic notes are a living
network you can think with. This is the intellectual foundation under your own
vault, atomic notes, your own words, dense linking, where the graph of connections
is the real asset. Understanding Zettelkasten explains why the discipline of small,
linked, self-authored notes matters more than just accumulating material.

## The Pattern
Write atomic notes in your own words and link them densely, so knowledge forms a
connected web that compounds. Connection over collection; the links are the value.
