# PKM Fundamentals

## What It Is
PKM stands for Personal Knowledge Management: the practice of capturing,
organizing, and connecting what you learn so it compounds instead of
evaporating. The goal is not to hoard notes but to build a system where past
thinking stays findable and useful, and where ideas link together into
something larger than any single note.

## How It Works
The core shift is from storing to connecting. A pile of notes is a filing
cabinet; a knowledge system is a graph, where notes link to related notes and
meaning lives in the connections. Most PKM methods share a few moves: capture
quickly so nothing is lost, process later into clean atomic notes each holding
one idea, and link generously so related thoughts find each other. The
Zettelkasten method is the classic example of this linked-note approach.

## Why It Matters
Knowledge that is only stored decays, because you forget you have it. Knowledge
that is connected stays alive, because you reach it through many paths. This is
the whole premise of your vault: notes are nodes, links are edges, and standard
names anchor your personal knowledge to the wider world so new learning attaches
to existing structure instead of forming isolated islands.

## The Pattern
Connection over collection. The value is not in how much you save, but in how
well it links.
